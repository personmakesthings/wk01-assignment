A Quick Look At The Many Ryokans (旅館) Of Japan

11th May, 2024. Written by John Smith, Tour Guide With Japan Tours.

Hey there Japan fans! Continuing our series looking at traditional Japanese leisure spots, today, we will be taking a look at the famous Japanese ryokan.

What is a ryokan, you might ask? Luckily, Wikipedia is here to give us a quick and handy overview:

"A ryokan (旅館) is a type of traditional Japanese inn that typically features tatami-matted rooms, communal baths, and other public areas where visitors may wear nemaki and talk with the owner."

Now lets take a further look...

The rooms you find in a ryokan are in a traditional Japanese style. In them, you will find tatami (畳) mats arranged for flooring, and sliding doors called fusuma (襖).

Ryokans are known for including food as part of their service for guests - a traditional Japanese bed and breakfast, if you like. The meals that are served are typically a mix of smaller dishes, often with locally sourced ingredients.

The robes you may see guests wear at these inns are known as nemaki (ねまき). Can you spot any similarities and differences nemaki has to other traditional Japanese clothes? (It's also hard not to see the appeal of the ryokan when the vibes are this cosy...)

Nearly all ryokan have common bathing areas segregated by gender, known as ofuro (お風呂). Those ryokan with hot springs nearby tend to source water from them.

Some of the more expensive ryokan overlook a beautiful, traditional Japanese garden. Very relaxing and scenic.

And that's it for today! I hope you have enjoyed this quick look into one of Japan's most famous types of inn!
